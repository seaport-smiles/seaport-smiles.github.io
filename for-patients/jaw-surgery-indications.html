Indications for
Orthognathic Surgery

Repositioning the facial bones to achieve maximum aesthetic balance and functional harmony.

The Foundation

Surgical Intervention

Orthognathic surgery is utilized principally for skeletodental syndromes that are beyond the control of conventional orthodontic procedures. While traditional orthodontics focuses mainly on tooth movement, surgery repositioning the facial bones adds a critical aesthetic dimension.

"Repositioning the facial bones moves the teeth into a stable relationship while maximizing soft tissue harmony."

Clinical Discrepancies

Class III (Prognathism)

Characterized by a prominent lower jaw or a deficient midface, often resulting in a concave profile.

Class II (Retrognathism)

Resulting from an underdeveloped mandible or downward jaw rotation, creating a recessed profile.

Comprehensive Care

Airway & Asymmetry

Beyond bite correction, jaw surgery is highly effective at correcting facial imbalances and expanding the physical airway for sleep apnea patients.
