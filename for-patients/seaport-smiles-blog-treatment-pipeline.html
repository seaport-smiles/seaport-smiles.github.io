Patient Guide

What actually happens between your consultation and your first aligner.

✍️ Dr. Osborn
🏥 Seaport Smiles
⏱ 6 min read
🦷 Invisalign

One of the most common questions we hear after a patient decides to move forward with Invisalign is some version of: "So… what happens now?" It's a fair question. There's a lot that happens between saying yes and putting in your first aligner — and most of it is invisible to the patient. This guide walks through every phase so you know exactly what to expect, what we need from you, and why each step matters.

01
Getting Started

Your New Patient Consultation

Your first visit is more than a meet-and-greet. Dr. Osborn will conduct a full clinical evaluation of your teeth, bite, and facial structure — and take all the diagnostic records needed to understand your case in detail.

This typically includes clinical photographs, X-rays, and a digital iTero scan of your teeth. The scan is one of the most important pieces: it creates a precise 3D model of your mouth that becomes the foundation for your entire treatment plan.

  • 📸
    Records taken: Clinical photos, panoramic and cephalometric X-rays, and a digital iTero scan
  • 💬
    What we discuss: Your goals, what Invisalign can achieve for your specific case, estimated treatment length, and financial options
  • 📋
    If you're ready to proceed: We'll finalize your contract and reserve a start date — so you leave with something on the calendar
⏱ Plan for 45–60 minutes for a thorough first visit
02
Important Step

Dental Clearance from Your Dentist

Before we begin building your treatment plan, we need a clearance form from your general dentist confirming that your teeth and gums are in good health. This is one of the most misunderstood steps in the process — so it's worth explaining carefully.

Why clearance comes first

Your iTero scan is only accurate if the physical shape of your teeth isn't going to change. If you have an untreated cavity, a crown in progress, or any restorative work pending, the shape of that tooth could change — which would invalidate your existing scan. We'd need to rescan and restart the treatment plan entirely. Getting clearance before we begin planning protects the accuracy of your aligners and avoids wasted time for everyone.

3
Weeks minimum before your start date We need clearance at least 3 weeks before your appointment — that's the minimum time required to plan your case and have your aligners manufactured and shipped.

We'll reach out to your dentist directly (or give you the form to forward). Most dentists turn it around within a few days. If there's pending work that needs to be addressed first, we'll let you know exactly what it is and follow up once it's resolved.

  • ✉️
    We send the form: We'll contact your dentist on file — or hand you the form to forward if that's faster
  • ⚠️
    If there's pending work: We'll tell you exactly what needs to be completed before we can move forward
  • 📬
    We follow up proactively: Our team monitors clearance for every patient with an upcoming start date — you'll hear from us before anything becomes a problem
⏱ Clearance typically takes a few days to 1–2 weeks depending on your dentist's schedule
03
Behind the Scenes

ClinCheck — Your Digital Treatment Plan

Once clearance is received, Dr. Osborn begins the clinical work. He submits a prescription to Invisalign's design team — a detailed set of instructions describing exactly how each tooth needs to move. Invisalign's CADCAM team then builds a digital simulation of your entire treatment: every stage, every tooth, from start to finish. This is called your ClinCheck.

When the initial design comes back, Dr. Osborn reviews every movement against your specific clinical goals. This review is detailed and deliberate — the goal isn't just to straighten teeth, it's to achieve the right result for your bite, function, and aesthetics.

Revisions are normal — and a good sign

In most cases, Dr. Osborn requests refinements from Invisalign's design team before approving. There are movements that require a back-and-forth with their technicians — and that process takes time. It's not a complication. It's what careful treatment planning looks like.

Once every movement is exactly right, Dr. Osborn completes the Case Review Sheet and gives final approval. At that point, your case enters fabrication.

  • 🔬
    Prescription submitted: Dr. Osborn sends detailed clinical instructions to Invisalign's design team
  • ✏️
    Review and refinement: The initial design is evaluated and revised until every tooth movement meets Dr. Osborn's clinical standard
  • ✅
    Final approval: ClinCheck approved, Case Review Sheet completed, case sent to manufacturing
⏱ Approximately 1 week for planning, review, and approval
04
Fabrication

Aligner Manufacturing & Delivery

After Dr. Osborn approves the ClinCheck, Invisalign manufactures your complete aligner series at their facility. Every aligner is precision-made from your exact iTero scan and the approved treatment plan — your unique tooth anatomy, your specific movements, your case. No two sets are the same.

Once manufactured, the full series ships via UPS directly to our office. We log the arrival in our system and notify you as soon as they're in.

  • 🏭
    Custom manufactured: Your entire aligner series is produced at once from the approved digital treatment plan
  • 🚚
    Shipped to us: Delivered directly to Seaport Smiles — we'll reach out the moment they arrive and confirm your start appointment
⏱ 10 business days from ClinCheck approval to delivery at our office
05
Active Treatment

Your Invisalign Start Appointment

Everything up to this point has been preparation. Your start appointment — we call it your ISB — is where treatment actually begins. Dr. Osborn will place any necessary attachments, fit your first set of aligners, and walk you through exactly how to care for them and get the most out of your treatment.

Active treatment involves returning every 6–8 weeks for progress checks. Between visits, you may submit photos through our Virtual Care portal so Dr. Osborn can monitor your progress remotely and catch anything that needs attention before your next in-office visit.

The single biggest factor in staying on track

Wear time. Aligners need to be worn 20–22 hours per day to work as planned. The movements in your ClinCheck are calculated based on consistent wear — skipping days or leaving aligners out extends your treatment timeline. It's the one variable entirely in your control.

  • 🔵
    Attachments: Small tooth-colored dots on specific teeth that help aligners apply more precise force — not everyone needs them
  • 📱
    Virtual Care: Remote progress monitoring between visits via photo submission through our patient portal
  • 🗓️
    Progress visits: In-office check-ins every 6–8 weeks — brief, efficient, and important
⏱ Active treatment: 6–18 months depending on case complexity
06
Final Phase

Retention — Protecting Your Results

Finishing treatment is a real milestone. But it's important to understand what comes next — because without retention, teeth have a natural tendency to drift back toward where they started. It's not a flaw in the treatment. It's just how teeth behave.

Retainers are what hold your result in place. At Seaport Smiles, we fit you for custom Vivera retainers — made by Invisalign from your final scan. They fit more precisely than generic retainers and are designed to last.

  • 🦷
    Vivera retainers: Custom-fit from your final scan — more precise and durable than off-the-shelf alternatives
  • 📅
    Wear schedule: Full-time initially, then nights only — Dr. Osborn will give you a specific protocol for your case
  • 🔄
    Replacement subscription: Retainers wear out over time. We offer a replacement program so you always have a fresh set — protecting your results long-term
🏆 Your smile is protected for life with consistent retainer wear
