Getting Started

Your New Patient Consultation

This is where your journey begins. Dr. Osborn will evaluate your teeth, bite, and facial structure, take diagnostic records including photos and X-rays, and discuss what Invisalign treatment would look like specifically for you.

📸
Records taken: Clinical photos, panoramic and cephalometric X-rays, and a digital iTero scan of your teeth
💬
What we discuss: Your goals, timeline, estimated treatment length, and what Invisalign can and can't do for your specific case
📋
If you're ready to proceed: We'll go over financial options and get your contract finalized — and we'll reserve your start date
⏱ 45–60 minutes for a thorough first visit
02
Step
📄
Important Step

Dental Clearance from Your Dentist

Before we begin building your treatment plan, we need confirmation from your general dentist that your teeth and gums are in good health. This isn't a formality — it's a clinical requirement that protects both you and the accuracy of your treatment.

Why This Matters

Your scan is only valid if your teeth aren't changing.

If you have an untreated cavity, a crown in progress, or any restorative work that could change the shape of a tooth, we'd need a new scan before starting. That resets the treatment plan entirely. We ask for clearance first so we don't waste your time — or ours.

3
Weeks minimum before your start date This is how long we need from the day we receive your clearance to have your aligners ready for your first appointment.
✉️
We'll send the form: We'll reach out to your dentist (or give you the form to forward) — it takes them just a few minutes to complete
⚠️
If there's pending work: We'll let you know exactly what needs to be addressed before we can move forward. Once it's done and re-cleared, you're good to go.
📬
We'll follow up proactively: Our team monitors clearance for every patient with an upcoming start date. You'll hear from us if anything needs attention.
⏱ Clearance typically takes a few days to 1–2 weeks depending on your dentist

Step
💻
Behind the Scenes

ClinCheck — Your Digital Treatment Plan

Once clearance is received, Dr. Osborn gets to work. He submits your prescription to Invisalign's design team, who build a digital simulation of your entire treatment — every tooth movement, every stage, from start to finish.

🔬
Dr. Osborn reviews every detail: The initial design comes back from Invisalign and Dr. Osborn evaluates each tooth movement against your specific clinical goals
✏️
Revisions are normal: In most cases, Dr. Osborn requests refinements from Invisalign's team before approving — this ensures precision, not just efficiency
✅
Final approval: Once every movement is exactly right, Dr. Osborn completes the Case Review and gives final approval to begin fabrication
⏱ Approximately 1 week for planning and approval
04
Step
📦
Fabrication

Aligner Manufacturing & Delivery

After Dr. Osborn approves your ClinCheck, Invisalign manufactures your custom aligner series at their facility and ships them directly to our office. Every aligner in your series is produced from your exact scan and Dr. Osborn's approved treatment plan.

🏭
Made to your exact specifications: Your aligners are precision-manufactured using your iTero scan and the approved digital treatment plan — no two sets are alike
🚚
Shipped via UPS to our office: Once manufactured, your full aligner series ships directly to Seaport Smiles — we'll notify you the moment they arrive
⏱ 10 business days from approval to delivery
05
Step
😁
Active Treatment

Your Invisalign Start Appointment

This is the moment everything has been building toward. At your start appointment, Dr. Osborn will place any necessary attachments, fit your first set of aligners, and walk you through everything you need to know to get the most out of your treatment.

🔵
Attachments (if needed): Small tooth-colored dots bonded to specific teeth that help aligners grip and move teeth with more precision
📱
Virtual Care check-ins: Between visits, you may submit photos through our Virtual Care portal so Dr. Osborn can monitor your progress remotely
🗓️
Regular progress visits: You'll return every 6–8 weeks for in-office progress checks — each visit is brief and efficient
⏰
Wear time matters: Aligners should be worn 20–22 hours per day. Consistent wear is the single biggest factor in staying on track with your timeline
⏱ Active treatment: 6–18 months depending on case complexity

Step
🏆
Final Phase

Retention — Protecting Your Results

Finishing treatment is a milestone — but it's not the finish line. Teeth have a natural tendency to shift back toward their original position. Retainers are what hold your result in place for the long term.

🦷
Custom Vivera retainers: Made by Invisalign from your final scan, these fit more precisely than generic retainers and are designed to last
📅
Wear schedule: Full-time wear initially, transitioning to nights only — Dr. Osborn will give you a specific protocol based on your case
🔄
Replacement program: We offer a retainer subscription so you always have a fresh set on hand — retainers wear out over time and should be replaced regularly
🏆 Your smile is protected for life with consistent retainer wear
Common Questions

Things patients
ask us all the time.

What happens if my dental clearance takes longer than expected?
+
If we don't receive your clearance at least 3 weeks before your reserved start date, we'll need to move your appointment. We'll always reach out proactively before this happens — you won't find out the day before. As soon as clearance comes in, we get you back on the schedule quickly.
Why do you need my dental clearance before starting the treatment plan?
+
If you have any pending dental work — a filling, crown, or extraction — it could change the physical shape of a tooth. That would invalidate your scan, which means we'd need to rescan and restart the treatment plan. Getting clearance first protects the accuracy of your aligners and saves everyone time.
Can I pick my start date?
+
Yes — we'll book a start date that works for you at the time of your contract signing. Just know that the date is a reservation until all the clinical pieces are in place: clearance received, ClinCheck approved, and aligners in the office. We'll keep you updated every step of the way.
Will I be able to see my treatment plan before I start?
+
Yes. Your ClinCheck is a digital simulation showing the projected movement of your teeth from start to finish. Dr. Osborn reviews and refines this before approval, and we can share a preview with you so you can see what your smile is expected to look like at the end of treatment.
What if I need refinements during treatment?
+
Refinements are a normal and often planned part of Invisalign treatment — they're additional aligners made mid-treatment to fine-tune your results. If refinements are needed, Dr. Osborn will take a new scan, submit an updated prescription, and order a new series of aligners. This adds some time but is part of achieving the best possible outcome.
How often do I need to come in during active treatment?
+
Most patients come in every 6–8 weeks for a progress check. Between visits, you may submit photos through our Virtual Care portal for remote monitoring. These check-ins are typically brief — just enough for Dr. Osborn to confirm everything is tracking as planned.
